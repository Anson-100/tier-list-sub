S Look at the criteria list. Based on these attributes, which submissions have the most maxed out stats? These are the techniques that belong in S-tier and every grappler should seek to become proficient in their use.

A A-tier submissions are highly effective and viable but usually have a slight drawback or limitation that keeps them out of S-tier.

B B-tier submissions are solid techniques however they are usually better-suited for competition use. They may not be the best option for neutralizing an opponent in a street encounter

C C-tier is the place for lower percentage moves. Some people may specialize in their use and have a lot of success with them but this is the exception rather than the rule.

D D-tier techniques are extremely low percentage and are usually pulled off only every now and then due to a rare opportunity. Learn D-tier submissions for fun but making them the cornerstone of your game is not recommended.

F These moves are trash.

-Strangles/chokes are usually better than joint locks.
Because an opponent may have enough pain tolerance, force of will, or drug-induced psychosis to to remain undeterred from having an arm broken or shoulder dislocated.
-Strangles where the arms wrap around just the head are usually better than strangles where the arms or legs wrap around a head and an arm.
Because strangles that wrap around just the head usually provide more focused pressure on the carotid arteries, making them more effective in cutting off blood supply to the brain.
-Rotational joint locks are usually better than straight joint locks.
Because they cause way more damage.
RANK THESE SUBMISSIONS BASED ON

  • EFFECTIVENESS

    How likely is this submission to neutralize an opponent in competition or the streets?

  • VERSATILITY

    is this submission effective against larger opponents? can it be applied from various positions?

  • RELIABILITY

    How consistently can the submission be successfully applied, even against skilled opponents?

  • RISK VS REWARD

    evaluate the potential gain against the potential exposure to counters or escapes
