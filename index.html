SLook at the criteria list. Based on these attributes, which submissions have the most maxed out stats? These are the techniques that belong in S-tier and every grappler should seek to become proficient in their use.

 

A A-tier submissions are highly effective and viable but usually have a slight drawback or limitation that keeps them out of S-tier.

 

B B-tier submissions are solid techniques however they are usually better-suited for competition use. They may not be the best option for neutralizing an opponent in a street encounter

 

C C-tier is the place for lower percentage moves. Some people may specialize in their use and have a lot of success with them but this is the exception rather than the rule.

 

D These are very low percentage moves that people may pull off every now and then due to a rare opportunity. Learn D-tier submissions for fun but making them the cornerstone of your game is not recommended.

 

F These moves are trash.

  • EFFECTIVENESS

    how likely is this submission to force a tap or neutralize an opponent in a competition or street encounter?

  • VERSATILITY

    is this submission effective against larger opponents? can it be applied from various positions?

  • RELIABILITY

    How consistently can the submission be successfully applied, even against skilled opponents?

  • RISK VS REWARD

    evaluate the potential gain against the potential exposure to counters or escapes
